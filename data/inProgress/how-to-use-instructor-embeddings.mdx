What Are Embedding Models?

Before diving into implementation, it's crucial to understand what embedding models are and why they are so valuable. Embeddings are a type of representation learning where entities (like words, products, or images) are mapped to vectors of real numbers. This section will explore the theory behind embedding models, highlighting their ability to capture the essence of data relationships in a low-dimensional space.

Types of Embedding Models:

Word Embeddings: Techniques like Word2Vec, GloVe, and FastText.
Image Embeddings: Using CNNs to reduce image dimensions.
Graph Embeddings: Techniques to encode graph structures into low-dimensional vectors.
Custom Embeddings: Tailored for specific industries or data types.
